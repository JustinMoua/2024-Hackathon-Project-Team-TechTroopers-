Elemental Alchemy and Transmutation

Temporal Mechanics and Time Travel Theory is a branch of physics that deals with the study of the

motion of objects through time, including the theoretical underpinnings

of time travel, its paradoxes, and its impact on the fabric of the universe.
We have a 4-year degree plan in Temporal Mechanics and Time Travel Theory that students can follow,

providing ample data regarding the intricate principles of navigating through time,

the mathematical foundations of temporal dynamics, and the physical laws governing the universe's

temporal fabric. This carefully crafted curriculum includes required courses in quantum physics,

general relativity, and temporal paradox resolution, alongside elective modules that explore the

ethical implications of time manipulation, historical case studies

in time travel, and advanced simulation techniques.
